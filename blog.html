Open Game Development!

April 10, 2024

After game engine unity screwed everybody, it looks like Slay the Spire 2 Will be the first big-deal game made with open source engine Godot. Maybe this shifts the needle on an entire software category; who knows. It's a good deal for everyone. The game studios don't need to deal with unity, so they get more money so they have less of a need to screw us over. Unity dies, but they're assholes so nobody gives a shit. The question then is who pays for the maintenance of Godot? Ideally the studios but that puts them all in a giant prisoner's dilemma. Then really ideally the state pays for it, but not really because making things is super cheap when all the workers are doing it for the common good and there are no middlemen or rent-seekers to pay. If godot gets captured and privatized into being a new Unity, we can expect them to get decadent and die too.

I would like to then also shout out Motion Twin, the creators of Dead Cells for running for over two decades as a worker's co-op. I love france.
