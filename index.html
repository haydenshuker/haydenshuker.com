UNDER CONSTRUCTION

To engineers and industry people: please redirect yourself to my linkedin.

To my friends and community: Where did all my stuff go? Short answer is I don't want my work indexed on the internet, especially not with my legal name. I'm giving up on web as a medium for expression. Making art for this site has been lonely and unrewarding. Being this public and this exposed kills the possibility of intamacy. I hate writing in linkedin voice or inoffensive-nothing-surrealism-voice because anybody could be watching, and I hate having my artistic past pile up behind me like bones. People don't want to engage with the web, the people who do are an anthropolicial curiosoity, waves of anti capital t Tech reaction unable to articulte a post internet culture. Its regressive and not exciting. I'm in Philadelphia, I'd rather get involved with real sceanes here. In my first post a few years ago I said by making the site I created a way for myself to be an artist. From the practice and feedback i've gotten doing this I now feel comfortable claiming that identity and can see a way forward more grounded than insistence into the void. I will be moving my work to new place under a psydonym, but know I don't care much about archiving myself nor do I care about web dev. I will turn this site into a proffessional facing portfolio page.

thank you for reading, see you all around.
